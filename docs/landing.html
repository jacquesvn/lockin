It's not a drill list. It's a plan that argues with you.

Anyone can hand you drills. The reason people don't improve is that they train hard for four days and then stop. Lockin is built around that failure, not around the drills.

One keystone

It picks the single habit costing you the most and makes it the spine of your week — instead of spreading you thin across eight things you'll half-do.

Ten minutes counts

A five-minute escape hatch runs one core drill and still marks the day. A skipped hour is worse than a short session, so the app makes the short one easy.

A cue, not a wish

"After dinner, I do the ten." Plans with a specific when get done; plans with a vague intention don't.

Never miss twice

Missing one day is noise. Missing two is how it ends. Miss yesterday and today's screen says so.

The gate

Match nights get a checklist — warmed up, not tilted, one process goal — and a stop-loss that calls it after two bad ones, before the third costs you more.

Death audit

Tag what actually killed you across a month and the pattern surfaces. Usually it isn't aim, and that's the point.

The coaching is checked, not vibes

Most CS advice is folklore repeated confidently. These are things Lockin gets right that common wisdom often doesn't — and the app will tell you when a metric stops being worth chasing.

Stopping

Your rifle's first shot is accurate below ~34% of max speed — a counter-strafe, not a dead stop. The AWP is stricter and wants a near-full stop.

Plateaus

Counter-strafing plateaus around 78%. Past that it stops separating players, so Lockin moves your reps to crosshair placement instead of pushing a number that no longer pays.

Ratings

Leetify Rating is roughly zero-sum — it's relative to your lobby. Chase the skill numbers underneath it, not the rating.

Maps

Map prep gives you the utility jobs and prefire routes, not step-by-step lineups. Exact throws are patch-specific, and a lineup that's quietly wrong is worse than none — so you save your own as you learn them.

No account. No server. No catch.

Lockin is one HTML file. Your plan, your streak and your numbers live in your browser's storage on your machine — there's nowhere for them to be uploaded, because there's no backend at all. Nothing to sign up for, nothing tracking you, and you can export the lot to a file whenever you like.

It's MIT licensed and the whole thing is on GitHub — including the tests that run before every release.
